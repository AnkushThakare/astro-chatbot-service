Common Astrology Concepts - Quick Reference

Kundali (Birth Chart / Horoscope)
A kundali is a map of the sky at the exact moment of a person's birth. It shows the positions of all nine planets (navagraha) across 12 houses and 12 zodiac signs. A kundali reading reveals personality traits, life patterns, strengths, challenges, and timing of major life events.

Requirements for generating a kundali:
- Exact date of birth
- Exact time of birth (as precise as possible)
- Place of birth (for latitude, longitude, and timezone)

The 12 Houses (Bhava)
1st House (Lagna/Ascendant): Self, personality, physical appearance, health
2nd House: Wealth, family, speech, food habits
3rd House: Siblings, courage, communication, short travel
4th House: Mother, home, property, vehicles, comfort
5th House: Children, education, creativity, romance, past-life merit
6th House: Enemies, diseases, debts, daily work, service
7th House: Marriage, partnerships, business, spouse
8th House: Longevity, sudden events, inheritance, transformation
9th House: Father, luck, dharma, long travel, higher learning, guru
10th House: Career, profession, status, public image
11th House: Gains, income, friends, aspirations, elder siblings
12th House: Losses, expenses, foreign travel, spirituality, moksha

Navagraha (Nine Planets)
Sun (Surya) - Soul, authority, father, government
Moon (Chandra) - Mind, emotions, mother, public
Mars (Mangal) - Energy, courage, property, siblings
Mercury (Budh) - Intelligence, communication, business
Jupiter (Guru/Brihaspati) - Wisdom, wealth, children, spirituality
Venus (Shukra) - Love, beauty, luxury, marriage
Saturn (Shani) - Discipline, karma, delays, longevity
Rahu - Illusion, foreign connections, obsessions
Ketu - Spirituality, detachment, past-life karma

Important Yogas (Combinations)
Raj Yoga: Combination promising power, authority, success
Gaja Kesari Yoga: Jupiter in kendra from Moon — wisdom and reputation
Mangal Dosha: Mars in 1st, 2nd, 4th, 7th, 8th, or 12th house — affects marriage compatibility
Kaal Sarp Dosha: All planets between Rahu and Ketu — struggles and sudden changes
Sade Sati: Saturn transiting over natal Moon (7.5 year period) — tests and transformation

Dasha System
The Vimshottari Dasha system divides a person's life into planetary periods:
- Each planet rules for a fixed number of years
- The dasha sequence depends on the Moon's nakshatra at birth
- Major periods (Mahadasha) are subdivided into sub-periods (Antardasha)
- Understanding current dasha helps explain what phase of life a person is in

Guna Milan (Matchmaking)
Traditional Vedic matchmaking compares two kundalis across 8 categories (Ashtakoota):
- Varna (1 point) - Spiritual compatibility
- Vashya (2 points) - Mutual attraction
- Tara (3 points) - Health and well-being
- Yoni (4 points) - Sexual compatibility
- Graha Maitri (5 points) - Mental compatibility
- Gana (6 points) - Temperament match
- Bhakoot (7 points) - Family and financial harmony
- Nadi (8 points) - Health and genetic compatibility

Total: 36 points. Generally 18+ points is considered acceptable for marriage.

Transit (Gochar)
Transits refer to the current positions of planets as they move through the zodiac, compared against the natal chart. Important transits include:
- Saturn transit (Shani Gochar) - changes every ~2.5 years per sign
- Jupiter transit (Guru Gochar) - changes every ~1 year per sign
- Rahu-Ketu transit - changes every ~1.5 years

Nakshatra (Lunar Mansions)
The 27 nakshatras are divisions of the zodiac based on the Moon's position. Each nakshatra has unique characteristics, ruling deity, and associated planet. Birth nakshatra determines the starting dasha and influences personality.
