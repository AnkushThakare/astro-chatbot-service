Common Vedic Astrology Concepts

A kundali is not a list of predictions — it is a map of the sky frozen at the exact moment you took your first breath. The positions of the nine planets (navagraha) across 12 houses and 12 zodiac signs at that precise second create a unique blueprint of your life's potential. To generate an accurate kundali, three things are essential: exact date of birth, exact time of birth (even a few minutes' difference shifts the lagna, which changes the entire chart), and the place of birth (for latitude, longitude, and timezone calculation). A kundali reveals personality patterns, natural talents, relationship dynamics, career aptitude, financial tendencies, and most importantly, the timing of when different life themes activate. Vedic astrology (Jyotish) is not about fixed destiny — it shows the terrain you are walking through and when the weather changes. Understanding your chart is like having a detailed travel map for life's journey.

The nine planets (navagraha) in Vedic astrology are not just celestial bodies — they are cosmic forces that shape personality and life events. Sun (Surya) governs your soul, authority, confidence, and relationship with father figures. Moon (Chandra) rules the mind, emotions, mother, and your inner world — in Vedic astrology, Moon sign matters more than Sun sign for daily predictions. Mars (Mangal) drives energy, courage, property matters, and physical vitality. Mercury (Budh) shapes intelligence, communication, business sense, and nervous system function. Jupiter (Guru) brings wisdom, wealth, children, and spiritual growth — it is the great benefic. Venus (Shukra) governs love, beauty, marriage, luxury, and creative expression. Saturn (Shani) teaches through discipline, delays, karma, and perseverance — feared but ultimately the most rewarding teacher. Rahu and Ketu are shadow planets (lunar nodes) representing worldly obsession and spiritual liberation respectively. Each planet's effect depends entirely on which house it sits in and which planets it aspects or conjoins.

The Vimshottari Dasha system is what makes Vedic astrology uniquely powerful — it answers not just "what" but "when." Life is divided into planetary periods that total 120 years. Each planet rules for a fixed duration: Ketu 7 years, Venus 20, Sun 6, Moon 10, Mars 7, Rahu 18, Jupiter 16, Saturn 19, Mercury 17. Your starting dasha is determined by the Moon's nakshatra at birth. The major period (mahadasha) is further divided into sub-periods (antardasha), creating a detailed timeline of which planetary energy dominates your life at any given time. When someone experiences sudden career growth, unexpected marriage, or a health crisis, the dasha almost always explains the timing. Understanding your current mahadasha and antardasha is like knowing which season of life you are in — it helps you make decisions aligned with planetary energy rather than fighting against it. A skilled astrologer reads the dasha alongside transits to pinpoint when specific life events are most likely.

Transits (gochar) are the current positions of planets moving through the zodiac compared against your birth chart. While dasha reveals which planet's energy dominates your life period, transit shows the day-to-day and month-to-month weather changes. Saturn changes sign approximately every 2.5 years, creating major life shifts wherever it transits. Jupiter moves through a sign in about 1 year, opening opportunities and expansion in that house's themes. Rahu and Ketu change signs roughly every 18 months, bringing obsessive focus and detachment to new life areas. The double transit theory says that when both Saturn and Jupiter simultaneously aspect the same house in your chart, that house's themes get powerfully activated — marriages happen, career changes occur, children are born. Transits are most impactful when they align with the running dasha. Saturn transiting your 10th house during Saturn mahadasha is a far more intense career restructuring than the same transit during Jupiter mahadasha.

Guna Milan (Ashtakoota matchmaking) is the traditional Vedic system for comparing two kundalis before marriage. Eight categories are scored totaling 36 points: Varna (spiritual compatibility, 1 point), Vashya (mutual attraction, 2 points), Tara (health compatibility, 3 points), Yoni (physical compatibility, 4 points), Graha Maitri (mental wavelength, 5 points), Gana (temperament match, 6 points), Bhakoot (family harmony, 7 points), and Nadi (genetic and health compatibility, 8 points — the most critical). A score of 18 or above out of 36 is generally considered acceptable. However, experienced astrologers look beyond just the number — Nadi dosha alone can override a high score, and Mangal Dosha analysis is done separately. Guna Milan is a starting filter, not the final answer. The 7th house lord placement, Venus condition, and current dasha timing for both partners matter just as much as the point score. Marriage compatibility is a multi-layered reading, not a single number.
