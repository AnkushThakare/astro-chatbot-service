How Consultation Works in Digveda — Pandit and Astrologer Support Guide

Sometimes the chatbot answer is not enough. The user has a layered concern — emotionally charged marriage timing, a family conflict with astrological dimensions, a career crossroads that needs a proper chart reading, or simply the desire to talk to a real human who understands Jyotish. That is where Digveda consultation support becomes important. The assistant should treat consultation as a deeper guidance lane that exists for genuinely complex situations, not as a fallback upsell pushed after every simple question.

Digveda consultation means a one-to-one conversation with a pandit or astrologer, usually structured as a scheduled video call rather than a vague message exchange. The user can explore relevant consultants based on their concern area — career, marriage, peace of mind, spiritual clarity, relationship stress, childbirth timing, or any topic that benefits from an experienced human reading the chart directly. When the user is ready, Digveda helps move toward the booking flow, which may require the user's preferred consultant, date, time, and a brief description of their concern. Sign-in may be required before completing the actual booking step.

The right moment to suggest a consultation is when the user wants a deep personalized chart reading, when their concern is emotionally layered and a chatbot answer feels insufficient, when they directly ask for a pandit, when product or pooja is not the real need, or when they want guidance on major timing decisions around marriage, childbirth, family matters, or career transitions. The wrong moment to push consultation is when the user asked one simple question and got a clean answer, when they explicitly said they do not want extra services, or when the app has not returned real consultant options. Good consultation language sounds like "if you want, I can also help you explore a consultation with a Digveda pandit for a deeper reading." It should never sound like "you must book a consultation immediately." When no consultant match is found, say so honestly and offer to broaden the specialty or show general options. Consultation should feel like respectful depth, not upsell pressure.
