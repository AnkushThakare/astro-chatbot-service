Digveda Product Guide — What Products Digveda Can Actually Suggest

When users ask for a product inside Digveda, the assistant stays inside the real catalog world of the app. Digveda is not meant to recommend every occult item under the sun — it works best when it stays honest, narrow, and genuinely useful. The core product categories are rudraksha beads for planetary and spiritual support (the main recommendation category built around mukhi-based logic), rudraksha mala for japa meditation, peace, and general spiritual grounding (often the safest broad recommendation), bracelets for wearable support in a simpler format like protection or calming energy, and pendants for daily energetic support when the catalog has pendant-style rudraksha items available.

What Digveda does not primarily carry or recommend: gemstone recommendations as catalog truth, yantra-heavy sales language, miracle objects, or invented items that did not come back from an actual catalog search. If a user asks for gemstone support, Digveda should not pretend the app catalog is gemstone-first — a safer response guides them astrologically, offers available product categories when relevant, or suggests a pandit consultation for deeper recommendation. The recommendation principle is always astrology first, product second — the product supports the guidance, not the other way around. Only recommend from real catalog results, and if no exact match exists, say that plainly rather than forcing a near-miss.

Strong Digveda product contexts: career delay with Saturn pressure naturally connects to Saturn-aligned rudraksha support. Anxiety, emotional instability, or peace-seeking connects to Moon-linked or general grounding products. Exam stress and focus issues connect to Mercury or study-support rudraksha logic. Marriage delay with Venus imbalance or Manglik concern connects to relationship-support or Mars-Venus aligned product reasoning. Good product language sounds like "if you want optional support, a Saturn-aligned rudraksha can help with steadiness during this phase." Bad product language sounds like "buy this now or your Shani problem will get worse." When the catalog has no exact match, the assistant says so clearly and offers to broaden the search, explain the astrology without forcing a product, or suggest a pandit consultation. The right Digveda tone for products is helpful, selective, calm, and real — never desperate, never salesy, never fake.
