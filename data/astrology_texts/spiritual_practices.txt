Planet-Wise Spiritual Practices and Remedies

Vedic astrology uses remedies to strengthen inner balance, not to create fear. The best remedies are simple, consistent, and connected to the planet's deeper principle. Mantra, fasting, charity, prayer, and disciplined conduct usually work better than dramatic one-time rituals.

General Rules for Remedies

- A remedy should support peace, clarity, humility, and self-discipline.
- Planetary remedies work best when done consistently over time.
- The current mahadasha, antardasha, and transit help decide which remedy is most relevant.
- Family tradition and personal devotion matter. A simple sincere practice is often stronger than a complicated one done without faith.

Sun Remedies

Sun represents vitality, authority, father, confidence, bones, and soul direction.

Best day: Sunday
Mantra: Om Suryaya Namah
Longer mantra: Om Hram Hrim Hraum Sah Suryaya Namah
Pronunciation note: Surya is commonly said as "Soor-ya".

Helpful practices:
- Surya Namaskar at sunrise
- offering water to the rising Sun
- keeping discipline and truthfulness
- honoring father, mentors, and authority rightly

Charity:
- wheat
- jaggery
- copper items
- red or saffron cloth

Moon Remedies

Moon represents mind, emotions, mother, sleep, nourishment, and inner security.

Best day: Monday
Mantra: Om Chandraya Namah
Longer mantra: Om Shram Shrim Shraum Sah Chandraya Namah
Pronunciation note: Chandra is commonly said as "Chun-dra".

Helpful practices:
- quiet prayer on Mondays
- moon gazing when emotionally calm
- keeping a gentle routine for food and sleep
- spending peaceful time with mother or maternal elders

Charity:
- rice
- milk
- white sweets
- white cloth

Mars Remedies

Mars represents courage, blood, conflict, property, younger siblings, and action.

Best day: Tuesday
Mantra: Om Mangalaya Namah
Longer mantra: Om Kram Krim Kraum Sah Bhaumaya Namah
Pronunciation note: Mangal is commonly said as "Mun-gul".

Helpful practices:
- Hanuman Chalisa
- disciplined exercise
- channeling anger into useful action
- avoiding unnecessary arguments

Charity:
- red lentils
- red cloth
- tools for workers
- food support to soldiers or athletes where appropriate

Mercury Remedies

Mercury represents intellect, speech, business, learning, logic, and communication.

Best day: Wednesday
Mantra: Om Budhaya Namah
Longer mantra: Om Bram Brim Braum Sah Budhaya Namah
Pronunciation note: Budha is commonly said as "Boo-dha".

Helpful practices:
- Vishnu prayer
- journaling, study, language practice
- speaking carefully and truthfully
- reducing scattered mental habits

Charity:
- green gram
- green vegetables
- stationery or books for students

Jupiter Remedies

Jupiter represents wisdom, teachers, children, ethics, faith, blessings, and expansion.

Best day: Thursday
Mantra: Om Gurave Namah or Om Brihaspataye Namah
Longer mantra: Om Gram Grim Graum Sah Gurave Namah
Pronunciation note: Brihaspati is commonly said as "Bri-hus-pa-ti".

Helpful practices:
- worship of Vishnu, Guru, or Dakshinamurti according to tradition
- serving teachers, priests, and elders
- studying sacred texts
- living with integrity and generosity

Charity:
- turmeric
- yellow dal
- yellow cloth
- bananas
- books or educational support

Venus Remedies

Venus represents relationships, beauty, comfort, art, vehicles, and refined pleasures.

Best day: Friday
Mantra: Om Shukraya Namah
Longer mantra: Om Dram Drim Draum Sah Shukraya Namah
Pronunciation note: Shukra is commonly said as "Shoo-kra".

Helpful practices:
- prayer to Lakshmi
- keeping home and clothing clean
- practicing gratitude in relationships
- using beauty and comfort without excess

Charity:
- white sweets
- curd
- rice
- white cloth
- support for girls, women, or marriage-related charity where appropriate

Saturn Remedies

Saturn represents karma, duty, delay, labor, endurance, justice, sorrow, and long-term stability.

Best day: Saturday
Mantra: Om Sham Shanicharaya Namah
Longer mantra: Om Pram Prim Praum Sah Shanicharaya Namah
Pronunciation note: Shani is commonly said as "Shuh-nee".

Helpful practices:
- Hanuman Chalisa
- serving the elderly, poor, sick, or laboring classes
- maintaining humility and punctuality
- regular prayer during Sade Sati or Saturn dasha

Charity:
- black sesame
- mustard oil
- black cloth
- shoes, blankets, or practical aid to the needy

Rahu Remedies

Rahu represents obsession, ambition, confusion, foreign influence, illusion, and unconventional paths.

Best day: Saturday or Wednesday depending on tradition
Mantra: Om Rahave Namah
Longer mantra: Om Bhram Bhrim Bhraum Sah Rahave Namah
Pronunciation note: Rahu is commonly said as "Raa-hoo".

Helpful practices:
- Durga prayer
- grounding meditation
- avoiding intoxicants and compulsive behavior
- keeping company with clear-minded people

Charity:
- dark blue or smoky-colored cloth
- sesame
- food donation to the poor

Ketu Remedies

Ketu represents detachment, moksha, sudden breaks, sharp intuition, and spiritual past karma.

Best day: Tuesday or Thursday depending on tradition
Mantra: Om Ketave Namah
Longer mantra: Om Sram Srim Sraum Sah Ketave Namah
Pronunciation note: Ketu is commonly said as "Kay-too".

Helpful practices:
- Ganesha worship
- meditation and silence
- spiritual study
- reducing ego attachment and confusion

Charity:
- multi-colored cloth
- blankets
- dog feeding in some traditions

Fasting Guidance

Traditional fasting days:
- Sunday for Sun
- Monday for Moon
- Tuesday for Mars
- Wednesday for Mercury
- Thursday for Jupiter
- Friday for Venus
- Saturday for Saturn and sometimes Rahu

Fasting should be sensible and health-conscious. If a person has medical conditions, strict fasting is not necessary. Simplicity, prayer, and self-control are more important than physical strain.

Temple and Deity Associations

Sun: Surya temples, Shiva temples at sunrise
Moon: Shiva or Devi temples, especially on Mondays
Mars: Hanuman or Subramanya temples
Mercury: Vishnu or Narayana temples
Jupiter: Vishnu, Guru, Dakshinamurti, or Sai traditions depending on family lineage
Venus: Lakshmi temples
Saturn: Shani temples, Hanuman temples
Rahu: Durga, Bhairava, or serpent-related worship depending on tradition
Ketu: Ganesha, Subramanya, or moksha-oriented Shiva worship

Meditation and Daily Spiritual Rhythm

For anxious minds:
- Moon mantra
- 5 to 10 minutes of breath awareness
- no screens immediately before sleep

For career pressure:
- Saturn or Sun remedy depending on the chart
- sunrise discipline
- one steady weekly seva or charity act

For relationship stress:
- Venus or Moon remedy
- gratitude prayer
- calmer speech on the planet's weekday

For spiritual growth:
- Jupiter or Ketu practice
- scriptural reading
- silent sitting at the same time every day

Core Principle

The strongest spiritual remedy is disciplined living. A mantra helps most when the person's speech, conduct, and daily routine begin to reflect the same planetary balance they are asking for.
